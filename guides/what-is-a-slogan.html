What is a Slogan?

Definition and 20 great examples of slogans used by well-known companies.
By Carolin Hewitt, Jun 21, 2024
Carolin is a Copywriter for Zarla. She has a background in marketing communication and brand building, and has written articles about branding and logo design for small businesses.

A slogan is a short and catchy phrase used to convey a business, brand, or product's appeal and entrench it in the consumer's mind. Slogans are typically used in advertising in conjunction with a brand or product name and can be commanding, humorous, persuasive, creative, descriptive, and/or emotive. FAQs:

What is an example of a slogan?

Examples of popular slogans include Nike's "Just Do It" and McDonald's "I'm lovin' it."

What are 5 popular slogans?

  • Gillette: "The Best a Man Can Get."
  • Subway: "Eat Fresh."
  • L'Oréal: "Because You're Worth It."
  • Toyota: "Let's Go Places."
  • Tesco: "Every little helps."

Whats does slogan mean?

A slogan refers to a short, memorable phrase commonly used in advertising to highlight the appeal of a business, brand, or product.

What is the best slogan?

There are many iconic slogans that have become inextricably linked with brands. The best slogans are timeless, concise, catchy, and create an instant link to the brand in consumers' minds, such as KFC's "It's Finger Lickin' Good" and L'Oréal's "Because You're Worth It."

What are good slogans?

Good slogans grab attention, are instantly recognizable, and are easy to remember. An example would be Skittles' "Taste the Rainbow."
