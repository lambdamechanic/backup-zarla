Motorcycle Website Examples - In-Depth Reviews of 5 Powerful Sites

Explore these 5 revved up motorcycle website examples that create a good visual appeal with impactful images and high-quality content.
By Carla Lottering, Jul 24, 2024
Carla is a Copywriter for Zarla based in Cape Town, South Africa. She has over 5 years' experience in online content creation and writes extensively on logos for small businesses.

Motorcycle websites act as virtual storefronts for both motorcycle enthusiasts and novices to find their perfect bike. As such, the websites should include bold design elements like dynamic typography and high-quality images to attract customers, along with diverse product pages and a seamless shopping experience to enhance the overall experience.

Website accessibility is also important to ensure visitors can easily find and read vital information, and optimization across both desktop and mobile will ensure the sites shine on various devices. To get started on your own website, use our motorcycle AI website builder to design a beautiful and impactful site in under a minute.

How We Evaluate Websites:

Zarla checks that sites meet basic web standards for mobile and desktop. We also use Google's PageSpeed Insights, and WAVE, a service that evaluates accessibility for individuals with disabilities. PageSpeed scores and accessibility are important factors for search engine rankings. PageSpeed scores may not match user experience and can vary substantially.

Alpha Motorcycles

https://www.alphamotorcycles.co.uk/

Essex, England, U.K.

Alpha Motorcycles is a Suzuki and Yamaha dealer that offers new demonstrator motorcycles, used bikes, and a fully equipped motorcycle servicing workshop. The website features appealing design elements, such as muted color accents and strong page layouts, to keep the company's branding consistent with ideas of power and authenticity.

Plenty of high-quality images advertising the dealer's diverse range of motorcycles ensure both amateur riders and professionals find their ideal bike on the website. Notable features include an online chat feature and a 3D walkthrough of the dealer's showroom on its "About" page.

Pros and Cons

The site's wide range of product categories appeals to a diverse customer base, while visitors also have access to ample page links expanding on the dealer's financing options, workshop services, and promotional offers. The addition of customer reviews adds a feeling of credibility to the site.

WAVE picked up several instances of contrast errors across the website, and according to accessScan, the site is non-compliant with assistive technologies. While most images on the home page are high-resolution, the image of the exterior showroom is quite pixelated and distracts from the site's appealing aesthetic.

Desktop Review

The desktop website presents as user-friendly and easily navigable with ample page links that allow visitors to find important info from the home page, including customer reviews, bike servicing options, and contact information. The site's page layouts are spacious and the good use of white space ensures that the content does not become overwhelming. On Google's PageSpeed Insights test, the site received a performance score of 83/100.

Mobile Review

On mobile, the website slims down nicely and condenses the images without losing resolution, contributing to a pleasant user experience. The site's headings remain prominent and the hamburger menu is easy to navigate, but the sticky customer reviews pop-up is quite large, which slightly hinders navigation. While real-world experience indicates fast page loading times, Google's PageSpeed Insights test scored the site's performance at 44/100.

Liberty CC Motorsports

https://libertycc.ca/

Dartmouth, Nova Scotia, Canada

Liberty CC Motorsports is a family-owned company that sells a wide variety of sports-related vehicles, including motorcycles, e-bikes, ATVs, and snowmobiles. The website incorporates straightforward design elements like a dark color palette and clean page layouts, along with casual-looking typography to emphasize specific headings and create a visually charming appeal.

The site features two top menu bars with page links directing visitors to the company's ample product categories, financing options, and contact information. Its company history and family legacy are also showcased on the "About" page, adding a feeling of authenticity to the website.

Pros and Cons

The website features a helpful search bar for users to easily find specific products and/or information, and the online product servicing inquiry process is intuitive. On WAVE, the website has only a few contrast errors, indicating great readability across all pages.

The website is missing dynamic visual elements like promotional videos or showroom walkthroughs to create a more engaging user experience. The lack of customer reviews and/or testimonials is also noticeable. accessScan indicates that the site is only semi-compliant with assistive technologies.

Desktop Review

The desktop version has a clean and professional look with lots of white space allowing the images and content, including the company's product range and categories, to shine. While pages load quite fast in real-time and Google's PageSpeed Insights test gave the site a performance score of 91/100, the test also indicated a lower-than-average accessibility score of 75/100.

Mobile Review

The mobile site displays all images and content well with spacious page layouts ensuring a refined appearance. However, the company logo becomes quite small on mobile and should be enlarged for a more prominent look.

Contrary to some competitor sites, the search bar and shopping cart are not hidden behind the hamburger menu and remain easily navigable in the top menu bar. Google's PageSpeed Insights test gave the website a score of 61/00 in the performance category.

Sol Invictus Motorcycle Co.

https://solinvictus.com.au/

New South Wales, Australia

Sol Invictus Motorcycle Co. specializes in manufacturing and customizing motorcycles made from high-quality materials in a "new vintage" style. The website lays out all important information, including the company's product range, custom parts, and workshop services, in a polished and spacious format.

The addition of several high-resolution images does a great job of advertising the various motorcycles on offer, and the "Stories with Sol" video is an engaging feature that gives customers insight into the company's capabilities and work philosophy.

Pros and Cons

The company offers a "Ride Now, Pay Later" financing option for budget-conscious customers, along with an FAQs section for additional info. The "Riders Journal" page profiles various motorcycles and their real-world performance, providing clients with a good overview of the company's products.

According to WAVE, the website has a large number of contrast errors, particularly between the footer menu's background color and the grey text. The top menu bar's drop-down list is difficult to read against the home page's hero image and would benefit from being displayed in a darker color.

Desktop Review

The desktop website features ample breathing room that allows visitors to easily navigate the various images and content, creating a smooth browsing experience. While the top menu bar is displayed prominently, additional icons like the search bar and shopping cart should be enlarged for optimal readability. On Google's PageSpeed Insights test, the desktop site received an adequate performance score of 69/100.

Mobile Review

The website performs well on mobile with fast page loading times, although Google's PageSpeed Insights test indicates much slower loading times, contributing to a lower performance score of 40/100. While the hamburger menu icon is quite small and easy to miss, it remains sticky as you scroll down, enhancing the overall user experience.

Onyx Moto

https://onyxmoto.com/

Lemon Grove, California, U.S.

Onyx Moto is a pre-owned motorcycle dealership that sells standard, sports, and dirt motorbikes from major manufacturers. The website offers an in-depth visual experience with high-quality images highlighting the dealership's large variety of products, as well as an introductory hero video depicting the showroom and workshop.

The company's inventory is easily searchable and includes pricing information to help users make informed purchasing decisions. The "About us" section provides a comprehensive overview of the dealership with various customer testimonials conveying both credibility and trustworthiness.

Pros and Cons

Motorcycles are categorized by type and brand, allowing customers to quickly and easily find their ideal bike. The "Text us" button gives users easy access to a team member for immediate assistance.

WAVE indicates a large number of contrast errors and missing image alt text throughout the website, and according to accessScan, the site is only semi-compliant with assistive technologies.

Desktop Review

The desktop website uses large headings, a sticky top menu bar, and informative page sections on the company's services to create a dynamic and easy-to-navigate desktop experience. Performance-wise, all pages load reasonably fast, and the site received a decent PageSpeed Insights score of 82/100. However, the test also indicated that the website's SEO practices could be improved upon.

Mobile Review

The mobile site functions adequately, with crisp images and well-organized content perfectly scaled for smaller screens. However, unlike the desktop version, the hamburger menu does not remain sticky. On Google's PageSpeed Insights test, the site received a low performance score of 34/100, but real-world experience indicates more reasonable page loading times.

Taverner Motorsports

https://www.tavernermotorsports.com.au/

Bowen Hills, Queensland, Australia

Taverner Motorsports is a Brisbane-based bike shop specializing in custom motorcycle builds, servicing, and repairs. The website uses hints of color with a traditional black-and-white color scheme to create visual interest and highlight the various page sections, which include information on the company's history, workshop services, and customer testimonials.

The "Gallery" page includes a collection of high-resolution images advertising the company's custom-built motorcycles, as well as a handy "Newsroom" section with blog posts on bike maintenance, vintage restorations, and engine guides. Together, these promote user engagement.

Pros and Cons

The site's FAQs section gives visitors insight into the company's bike servicing and repair processes. The online quote request form is intuitive and its additional contact options with a location map let users know exactly where the shop is located. According to WAVE, the website has no contrast errors.

Some of the website's body text, including its footer menu text, is displayed quite small and should be enlarged for optimal readability. accessScan also notes that the site does not include any sort of accessibility system.

Desktop Review

On desktop, the website has spacious layouts, which ensures that its content-rich pages are easily readable and navigable. The sticky top menu bar contributes to a smooth browsing experience, but the addition of more enticing visual elements, such as videos or 3D walkthroughs, would increase user interest tenfold. On Google's PageSpeed Insights test, the website scored 69/100 in the performance category.

Mobile Review

Like the desktop version, the mobile website features clean and well-spaced page layouts, but its body text is displayed more prominently, enhancing the overall reading experience. The hamburger menu remains sticky and the "Get a Quote" CTA button is noticeable in the top menu bar. While real-world tests indicate fast page loading times, Google's PageSpeed Insights test noted much slower loading times and gave the site a score of 33/100 for performance.
