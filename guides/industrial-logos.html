  2. If you see a design you like, you can click on the logo to experiment with different colors, icons, and typefaces.
  3. Research the tools, machinery, and products of your industrial field to find elements that will work well in your logo.

How can I make a vintage industrial logo?

  1. Use high-quality, old-style typefaces to give your design an artisanal feel.
  2. Textured colors like rusty orange, dusty charcoals, and pale browns hint at the use of well-worn materials.
  3. Classic icons that incorporate founding dates, banners, smokestacks, and hand tools can help capture a sense of industrial nostalgia.

What are some steel industry logo design ideas?

  1. Clean, all caps typefaces are structured, professional, and striking.
  2. Simple, no-frills color schemes create a distinguished and timeless impression.
  3. Cogs and mechanical icons work well to create catchy and powerful images.
  4. A balanced composition will convey a sense of precision and harmony.

What colors should I use for my manufacturing logo?

Simple colors work well to communicate a sense of professionalism and reliability. Grays and blacks resonate with the materials and machinery in a manufacturing plant and allow lighter icons to stand out. Royal blues, golden yellows, and rusty oranges are sophisticated options that may differentiate your design.
