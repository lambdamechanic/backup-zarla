Church Website Examples - 5 Holy Sites That Engage Visitors

Learn how to create an impactful and user-friendly website for your church or ministry with our review of 5 church website examples.
By Carla Lottering, Jul 10, 2024
Carla is a Copywriter for Zarla based in Cape Town, South Africa. She has over 5 years' experience in online content creation and writes extensively on logos for small businesses.

Church websites serve as virtual spaces for visitors to access the ministry's online resources, including livestreamed sermons, community meetups, and prayer requests. Page sections expanding on the church's leadership team, weekly sermon times, faith statements, and donation options will inspire users to get involved with the ministry's congregation.

Simplifying important information and breaking up text-heavy sections with visual elements will create a coherent and pleasant user experience. Get started on your own website with our church AI website builder, which will generate a clean, intuitive site complete with content in under a minute.

How We Evaluate Websites:

Zarla checks that sites meet basic web standards for mobile and desktop. We also use Google's PageSpeed Insights, and WAVE, a service that evaluates accessibility for individuals with disabilities. PageSpeed scores and accessibility are important factors for search engine rankings. PageSpeed scores may not match user experience and can vary substantially.

Mariners Church

https://www.marinerschurch.org/

Irvine, California, U.S.

Mariners Church offers weekly worship experiences for members at its seven California-based locations and via live streaming. The website features a professional design with tasteful color accents and clean page layouts that highlight the images and videos of church members engaging in worship, baptism, and sharing personal testimonies.

The content-rich pages are well-organized to ensure visitors can easily find the church's locations, watch sermons, and access additional resources like prayer requests and marriage counseling. The site also places an emphasis on community engagement with a variety of group events and volunteering opportunities listed.

Pros and Cons

The website's ample page links give users a comprehensive overview of the church's ministries, leadership team, outreach events, and social media engagement. The addition of interactive elements like videos and live streaming options enhance the overall user experience.

WAVE picked up several instances of contrast errors, empty headings, and missing image text across the website. According to accessScan, the site is only semi-compliant with assistive technologies.

Desktop Review

The desktop version presents as user-friendly with a decent performance score of 84/100 on Google's PageSpeed Insights test. On desktop, the ample white space and clear visual hierarchies allow visitors to easily explore the site's content offerings, access service times and programming details, and get involved with the church. However, some of the pages hidden behind the hamburger menu should be moved to the top menu bar for a smoother browsing experience.

Mobile Review

The website slims down nicely to fit all content and images to smaller screens, creating a cohesive and easy-to-navigate mobile experience. The home page's videos play without fault on mobile, and while all pages have spacious layouts, some text-heavy sections could be broken up with additional images for a better visual impact. On Google's PageSpeed Insights test, the website scored 42/100 in the performance category.

Liverpool Cathedral

https://liverpoolcathedral.org.uk/

Liverpool, England, U.K.

Liverpool Cathedral is a Christian church that offers daily worship services and cathedral tours. The website captures the grandeur of the cathedral with plenty of high-quality color images depicting its interior, and visitors can easily explore the church's offerings with sections on its community activities, music programs, and venue hire options.

The use of prominent color blocks across all pages contributes to a clear visual hierarchy, and a standout feature is the home page's "I want to" drop-down menu that takes visitors to specific pages based on their selection.

Pros and Cons

The "How to find us" section on the home page gives visitors a good idea of where the cathedral is located with written directions and a map. Users also have easy access to the church's various activities with a dedicated "Upcoming Events" page.

The website does not feature any video or livestreamed content for users wanting to access the church's worship services online. WAVE also picked up a large number of contrast errors across the site, particularly between the top menu bar's text and its background.

Desktop Review

The desktop website solidifies the church's online presence with easy-to-navigate pages expanding on its history, various services, and opening hours. The use of high-resolution, fast-loading images adds to the site's visual appeal, but the lack of a floating top menu bar is noticeable. Performance-wise, the website has a decent score of 90/100 on Google's PageSpeed Insights test.

Mobile Review

The mobile version retains its polished appearance with a legible text size and crisp images fit to the smaller screen, although site navigation is slightly cumbersome without a floating top menu bar or a back-to-top button. According to Google's PageSpeed Insights test, the website has lower-than-average page loading times, indicated by a performance score of 50/100.

Joy Fountain Church

https://joyfountainchurch.com/

Winnipeg, Manitoba, Canada

Joy Fountain Church is a Winnipeg-based church that provides in-person and live streamed Sunday worship services to new and existing congregation members. The website embraces simplicity with clean design elements, such as a muted color scheme and minimal imagery, to allow its content on faith, prayer, and community to shine.

The "About Us" section introduces visitors to the church's leadership team and beliefs, while ample page links on various age-specific programs appeal to a family-friendly audience.

Pros and Cons

The website features testimonials from congregation members, denoting a feeling of authenticity. Prominent CTA buttons allow visitors to easily submit prayer requests or praise reports, as well as donate to the church via various payment options.

accessScan indicates that the site is only semi-compliant with assistive technologies. WAVE also noted several contrast errors and missing image alt text throughout the website, indicating suboptimal readability.

Desktop Review

On a desktop, the website's content and images have ample breathing room with lots of white space and well-organized layouts, ensuring smooth navigation. However, some contrast ratios could be increased to give several text sections better readability, such as the footer menu. On Google's PageSpeed Insights test, the site scored 99/100 in the performance category, indicating optimal page loading times.

Mobile Review

While the website's content fits nicely on mobile screens, several images on the home page do not display correctly, leading to a less impactful visual experience. While many CTA buttons remain prominent on mobile, the "Give" button gets hidden behind the hamburger menu. The site scored 85/100 in the performance category on Google's PageSpeed Insights test.

Australia for Christ Church

https://australiaforchrist.church/

Rowville, Victoria, Australia

Australia for Christ Church offers in-person and online Sunday services in various languages, including Mandarin and Indonesian. The website lays out essential information, including the church's service hours, upcoming events, and youth programs in a visually appealing format with plenty of white space.

The site uses emotionally evocative language to welcome visitors to the church, and high-quality images of its pastors, sermons, and congregation members do a great job of conveying the church's commitment to faith and community.

Pros and Cons

Users can easily access the site's archive of sermons with downloadable notes. The "Our Senior Pastors" page gives the website a personable touch with comprehensive information on the church's leadership team.

The website does not offer a search bar for easy browsing, and the social media links set against the home page's hero image are easy to miss. According to WAVE, the site has several contrast errors.

Desktop Review

The desktop site performs well and real-world experience indicates decent page loading times, although Google's PageSpeed Insights test notes slow loading times. The addition of favored navigation options like a back-to-top button contributes to smooth browsing, but the inclusion of more striking visuals would enhance the overall user experience. The website has a Google PageSpeed Insights performance score of 86/100.

Mobile Review

While the mobile site displays all content and images without losing functionality, the excessive use of white space under the home page's hero image is slightly distracting. Contrary to its desktop counterpart, the website's top menu bar does not remain sticky. Google's PageSpeed Insights test gave the site a performance score of 39/100 and indicated slower-than-average page loading times.

Emmanuel Faith Community Church

https://www.efcc.org/

Escondido, California, U.S.

Emmanuel Faith Community Church is a local congregation that offers weekly sermons both in-person and livestreamed. The website presents a good visual experience with an introductory hero video depicting the church's services and community members, along with plenty of high-quality images and audio tools available to visitors interested in its sermons and devotional podcasts.

The website features ample page links that give visitors insight into the church's leadership team, educational programs, community events, and its downloadable app for parishioners on the go. A notable feature is the site's Spanish translation tool, which aids user accessibility.

Pros and Cons

The website's "How Can We Help?" dropdown list is a helpful feature for users in search of specific information, and the "Calendar" page gives visitors a good overview of the church's events schedule.

The church's logo is displayed quite small on both desktop and mobile and should be enlarged for increased brand recognition. WAVE picked up several instances of insufficient contrast ratios across the website.

Desktop Review

The desktop website has a professional and refined appearance with large headings that ensure visitors can easily find vital information, such as the church's service times, counseling options, and sermon videos. The use of dark color accents and ample white space creates a satisfactory visual experience, but some pages load quite slowly and should be optimized for smoother browsing.

Mobile Review

On a mobile, the home page's hero video plays without fault and all images remain refined, although they appear much smaller than on desktop. Lots of important information, such as the Spanish translation tool and search bar get hidden behind the hamburger menu, and should be added to the top menu bar for additional prominence. On Google's PageSpeed Insights test, the site has a performance score of 34/100, although real-world experience is more intuitive.
