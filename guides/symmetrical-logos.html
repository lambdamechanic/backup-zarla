What is a symmetrical logo?

A symmetrical logo is a design that is generally mirrored around the vertical axis to produce a left and right mirrored image. However, symmetry can be achieved across multiple axes in shapes such as circles and stars. Details, such as color and form, can be used to accentuate symmetrical logo designs.

Does a logo need to be symmetrical?

A logo does not need to be symmetrical. Depending on the industry, asymmetrical logos can work well to create a unique and intriguing brand identity. However, for fields such as engineering, beauty, and design, a symmetrical logo design can be highly appealing and convey notions of precision, balance, harmony, and proportion.

How do you make a logo symmetrical?

In order to make a logo symmetrical, you'll need to come up with a design, halve it, and then flip the halved element so that it forms a mirrored image where both sides are exactly the same.

What is a symmetrical shape?

A symmetrical shape is a shape that is mirrored across one or more axes. Generally, all regular shapes are symmetrical. For example, triangles, circles, squares, and stars are all symmetrical forms.
