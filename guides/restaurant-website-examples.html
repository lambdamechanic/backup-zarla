Restaurant Website Examples - 5 Delicious Sites Reviewed In Depth

9 visually appealing restaurant website examples guaranteed to bring in customers.
By Simoné Gambrell, Jul 23, 2024
Simoné Gambrell is a Copywriter based in South Africa. She has enjoyed using her creativity to research and write articles about logos for small businesses since 2019.

A restaurant website is the perfect platform to share your restaurant's history, personality, and values. The sites should include your restaurant's contact information, exciting and compelling images of the food, and a copy of the menu. It's a bonus if the website includes online ordering or reservation features.

How We Evaluate Websites:

Zarla checks that sites meet basic web standards for mobile and desktop. We also use Google's PageSpeed Insights, and WAVE, a service that evaluates accessibility for individuals with disabilities. PageSpeed scores and accessibility are important factors for search engine rankings. PageSpeed scores may not match user experience and can vary substantially.

The Shed

https://sfshed.com/

Santa Fe, New Mexico, U.S.

The Shed Restaurant, located in Santa Fe, is known for its New Mexican cuisine, mixing Pueblo, Spanish, and Mexican styles. The site features earthy, warm tones, handwritten-style typefaces, and high-quality images that highlight the Mexican theme of the restaurant in a visually pleasing design.

The restaurant's menus are available on the site, rather than as a PDF download which often shows issues on mobiles, and customers can easily find information on location and hours. A good bonus for customers is the site's online store, which offers gift cards and herbs and sauces.

Pros and Cons

The site highlights the restaurant's culture and history, as well as the online store with gift cards, and has a simple navigation. It also ranks well on Google's PageSpeed Insights report for both mobile and desktop sites.

The home page lacks a visual hierarchy as the important elements don't stand out, and the site is only semi-compliant with assistive technologies. WAVE reports around 20 missing alternative text, empty header, and contrast errors on the site.

Desktop Review

The desktop site has a spacious layout and the images pop against the light, earthy background. The food menus are well-organized, making them easy to read, and the online store offers a simple design that is particularly easy to use on a desktop. According to PageSpeed Insights, the desktop site rates in the 90s for performance, accessibility, and SEO practices, with a score of 100/100 for best practices.

Mobile Review

This site is well laid out on smaller screens, keeping the earthy tones, clear images, and a simple menu layout. The verticle layout means that visitors to the site will need to scroll a lot, but the layout also ensures all text, particularly the food menus, is clearly legible. However, the site does feature large content that could be streamlined and has a 73/100 performance rating on Google's PageSpeed Insights report.

The Porch Restaurant and Bar

https://theporchrestaurantandbar.com/

Sacramento, California, U.S.

The Porch Restaurant and Bar in Sacramento features Southern cuisine with a Californian twist. The restaurant delivers on Southern charm and hospitality, and this theme is reflected on its website, which welcomes viewers with an enticing video showing food and drink preparation.

Visitors to the site will be pleased to see information on catering and large parties, and the reservation button is clearly highlighted. The site also offers customer reviews to build trust. However, while there is a page for online ordering, this function is actually not available.

Pros and Cons

The clean, minimalist design aids in navigation, and the site is fully compatible with assistive technologies. WAVE also notes just 1 contrast issue on the site. By highlighting specials and images of the location and food, the site encourages patrons.

PageSpeed Insights reports that the desktop and mobile sites both have slow loading times. The site's online ordering function is currently not available, and there are currently only three items available for online ordering, creating wasted space.

Desktop Review

The desktop site welcomes visitors with a high-quality video banner taking up the whole visible space and enticing customers with delicious food. The social media links in the top menu are clearly visible and encourage visitors to click over. This site scores 100% for accessibility on Google's PageSpeed Insights report, but due to slower loading times, only has a performance rating of 61/100.

Mobile Review

The mobile site looks just as good as the desktop, with the layout being optimized for the smaller screen. The mobile version appears to have a warmer color palette, but the hamburger menu maintains the easy navigation of the desktop site. On PageSpeed Insights report, the mobile site maintains high accessibility, best practices, and SEO scores, but due to the very slow loading speeds noted, the mobile site scored 28/100 on performance.

Gather in Omaha for Food and Drink

https://gather-omaha.com/

Omaha, Nebraska, U.S.

Gather in Omaha offers a farm-to-dining experience highlighting fresh produce harvested from their hydroponic vertical urban farm. The site itself has an urban, contemporary aesthetic with a charcoal gray and white color palette that features touches of green to enhance the organic aspect.

An interactive 3D map allows visitors to virtually tour the restaurant and farm, and the addition of customer reviews and dining awards increases customer trust. The layout of the site may come across as cramped on certain screens, as the site lacks enough white space for easy visibility and is information-dense.

Pros and Cons

The website reflects the restaurant's aesthetic and offers high-quality visuals and easy access to reservations, events, and gift cards. WAVE reports only a handful of contrast and empty link errors.

Long blocks of text and a lack of white space make the site look cramped, especially with the menu oriented vertically on the left of the page. The site is not compliant with assistive technologies.

Desktop Review

The desktop website shines with a well-laid-out menu down the left side of the screen instead of the traditional top-of-the-screen menu, creating visual interest. On Google's PageSpeed Insights report, the desktop site rates 80/100 for accessibility, leading to a performance score of 88/100.

Mobile Review

The mobile site is well-optimized for smaller screens, but streamlining the text-rich sections would improve loading times. According to PageSpeed Insights, the mobile site has a slow speed index, leading to a performance rating of 66/100, despite the mobile site's accessibility score being higher than the desktop's.

Syrahs of Jasper

https://syrahsofjasper.com/

Jasper, Alberta, Canada

Syrahs of Jasper is a restaurant located in Jasper that features regional Canadian cuisine that is almost entirely gluten-free. The restaurant also caters to various other dietary preferences.

The site is sophisticated, professional, and minimalistic, with a well-spaced layout, a simple menu, and customer reviews and awards. Visitors to the site can easily make a reservation online and view the menu, but the site lacks information on events and large bookings and would benefit from online ordering or a loyalty program.

Pros and Cons

The elegant site features a well-spaced layout with enticing images and good descriptions of the food. The site includes an efficient reservation system and WAVE reports less than 5 errors on the site, including an empty heading and contrast issues.

The site is not compliant with assistive technologies and loads slowly on both desktop and mobile devices. The contact details are only available in small text that shows weak contrast, leading to poor visibility.

Desktop Review

On the desktop version, the food and drinks menu is well laid out, taking advantage of the available space, but some textual elements could be made more concise. PageSpeed Insights notes that the best practices could be improved, and an overall performance score of 94/100 is awarded.

Mobile Review

The mobile site is well-optimized for the smaller screen, keeping a consistent color palette, elegance, and easy navigation. However, due to the smaller screen, the food and drinks menu requires a lot of scrolling, and some text blocks are very long. The load time is slower on the mobile, leading to a performance rating of 41/100 on Google's PageSpeed Insights report.

Sunset Grill

https://sunsetgrill.ca/

Saint Johns, New Brunswick, Canada

Sunset Grill is a breakfast and lunch restaurant dedicated to fresh ingredients and healthy cooking methods. The good use of white space makes the site feel spacious and comfortable.

The food menu is available on the site and as a PDF download, depending on the location, but the online menu is divided into an unnecessary number of categories which means customers will have to do a lot of scrolling and clicking. Customers will enjoy the addition of gift card information and FAQs.

Pros and Cons

The simple design creates a responsive website that is easily navigated and adapts well to various screens. WAVE reports only a handful of link and contrast errors on the site. The rotating hero banner advertises the restaurant's foods and specials with mouthwatering images.

The site is only semi-compliant with assistive technologies and is slow to load, according to PageSpeed Insights. The site also lacks a reservation feature and the social media links may be hard to see.

Desktop Review

The desktop site makes use of the generous white space to create a comfortable viewing experience. Large images and small blocks of text increase the visual aesthetic of the site without overwhelming the viewer. Google's PageSpeed Insights report awards the desktop site a score of 100/100 for best practices and 95/100 for accessibility. However, slower loading times and large content bring the overall performance rating down to 65/100.

Mobile Review

The mobile site remains consistent with the desktop, maintaining a clean layout and clear navigation. The best practices score on PageSpeed Insights is slightly lower for the mobile site, while the accessibility score is 99/100. The speed index is considerably lower, leading to an overall performance score of 38/100.

Casey's Grill Bar

https://www.caseyskenora.com/

Kenora, Ontario, Canada

Casey's Grill Bar is located in Kenora and features a pub-style menu and friendly atmosphere. The site's clean layout and easy navigation ensure customers can find all essential information with no distractions from the menu, location, and contact information.

However, the site would benefit from more images of the food and location, as well as online reservation software, customer reviews, and large booking or catering information. Visitors to the site will benefit from a loyalty program.

Pros and Cons

The site has very positive results on PageSpeed Insights for both desktop and mobile, WAVE reports less than 5 errors on the site, and it is compatible with assistive technologies. The "Daily Features" page is an enticing addition to the site.

The simple design of the site lacks customer engagement, missing images and interactive elements, such as a blog or reservation software. The desktop site lacks a map of the restaurant's location to help customers find them through GPS.

Desktop Review

The site presents well on a desktop, with plenty of white space creating a comfortable, streamlined user experience. The traditional menu is clear and allows for easy navigation, and the accessibility assistance feature ensures all visitors can access the site. Google's PageSpeed Insights report gives the desktop site an overall performance rating of 91/100, with the SEO, best practices, and accessibility rating of 100% each.

Mobile Review

On mobile devices, the layout is rearranged while keeping a simple design structure and clear menu. The mobile site includes a GPS map and a link for visitors to find directions, which is lacking on the desktop site, but has a pop-up urging customers to become VIP members, which may be seen as pushy.

While the mobile site maintains high scores for SEO and best practices on PageSpeed Insights, the slow loading times bring the overall performance rating down to 54/100.

Hendrix

https://www.hendrixbelfast.co.uk/

Belfast, Northern Ireland, U.K.

Hendrix specializes in modern food and elaborate cocktails and offers customers a warm and welcoming atmosphere, which is reflected in their website. A color palette of blue and golden orange highlights this sense of welcome, while the clear navigation brings the CTAs to the forefront and the abundant use of white space offers clean legibility.

The site offers a ribbon of high-quality images, with a link to their Instagram page for more images, but the images on the site itself feature the same two plates. Two of the menus are in PDF format, which doesn't always download well on mobile devices.

Pros and Cons

The site is user-friendly with high-quality images and prominent CTAs that encourage customers to call the restaurant for bookings or find directions to the location. WAVE reports no errors on the site.

The site lacks variety in its images and visual interest and is limited in the information provided, for example, there is no history about the restaurant. The site is only semi-compliant with assistive technologies.

Desktop Review

The desktop site is well suited to larger screens, with a strong grid layout and larger text. The spaciousness of the design ensures users can easily explore the menus and make reservations with ease. On Google's PageSpeed Insights report, the desktop site has a strong accessibility score of 94/100, but slightly slower loading times lower the overall performance score to 67/100.

Mobile Review

The easy navigation and clear layout remain constant on the mobile site, keeping the strong contrast between text and background that increases legibility. The site is well-optimized for the smaller screen, but the food and drinks menus may appear somewhat cramped. PageSpeed Insights reports slow loading times leading to an overall performance rating of 36/100.

Urban Greek

https://urbangreek.com.au/

Hobart, Tasmania, Australia

Urban Greek features an authentic Greek menu in the heart of Tasmania, offering traditional family recipes with a modern twist. The site features a full hamburger menu that offers users the opportunity to find all essential information, including menus, online ordering, large group bookings, and online reservations.

The site also features some additional information, like vouchers, job opportunities, customer reviews, and an interactive restaurant view to increase user engagement. The high-quality images and clear CTAs entice customers to try your food.

Pros and Cons

The site is user-friendly and responsive, only featuring 2 empty links and some contrast errors, according to WAVE. The clear and in-depth hamburger menu provides visitors with all the information they need to know about this restaurant.

The site is only semi-compliant with assistive technologies and needs to improve its performance, as both the mobile and desktop sites scored under 70% on PageSpeed Insights. With the amount of information available on the site and the upcoming events, the site needs a newsletter.

Desktop Review

The desktop site features a clean grid layout that aids with readability and the images are shown to full effect on the larger screens, enticing customers with images of the food. However, the desktop site only has a performance rating of 63/100 on Google's PageSpeed Insights, due to lower accessibility and best practices scores. According to PageSpeed Insights, the desktop site lacks discernable names on some links, uses third-party cookies, and lacks descriptive text on two links.

Mobile Review

The mobile site maintains enough white space to keep the clean legibility and layout of the site. The hamburger menu and CTAs remain constant across the desktop and mobile versions and the images remain good quality. The mobile site has the same accessibility issues as the desktop, according to PageSpeed Insights, but slower loading times lead to an overall performance score of 44/100.

Black Fire Restaurant

https://blackfirerestaurant.com/

Brisbane, Queensland, Australia

Black Fire Restaurant, also known as Fuego Nero, features a Spanish and Mediterranean menu served in a welcoming atmosphere. The drop-down menu design is interesting, showing the different menus in the middle of the hero image rather than right below the top menu. Moreover, the PDF files of the menus may lead to difficulties on mobile devices.

Visitors to the site will appreciate the clear location, contact details, and listed opening hours, as well as the strong CTAs. Information about vouchers is nice to have, but the site could benefit from information on group bookings and online ordering.

Pros and Cons

The high-quality images create a stunning visual experience and WAVE reports only a handful of empty link and contrast errors. The site also consistently ranks highly on the PageSpeed Insights report.

The site is only semi-compliant with assistive technologies and the page load speed needs to be optimized. The food and drinks menus are available as PDF downloads, which often show issues on mobile devices.

Desktop Review

The site shines on larger screens, especially the high-quality images and prominent CTAs. On Google's PageSpeed Insights report, the desktop scores 100/100 for both best practices and SEO, and 91/100 for accessibility, while it achieved an overall performance score of 93/100.

Mobile Review

The mobile website looks good on smaller screens, maintaining clean legibility, easy navigation, visible CTAs, and high-quality images. The mobile site maintains the full best practices and SEO scores on PageSpeed Insights, with an accessibility rating of 90/100 and slower loading speeds leading to an overall performance score of 84/100.

What is a restaurant website?

A restaurant website acts as a digital storefront to tell visitors to the site about the restaurant, allow them to make reservations or bookings, view the menu, and find information about events or large bookings.

What should be on the home page of a restaurant website?

Your home page should entice customers to visit your restaurant by providing a simple overview of your brand story, food, team, and philosophy. You should also have your contact information, a reservation link, and a link to your menu.

Which website builder is best for a restaurant?

When choosing a website builder for a restaurant site, you should look for a content management system (CMS) that suits your needs and pre-made templates that you can customize without any coding knowledge. Also try to find a builder that will incorporate reservation software on your site.

What should my restaurant website design include?

  • The restaurant's story, history, philosophy, and team information.
  • Information about the type of food served.
  • The menu.
  • Reservation software.
  • Clear contact information.
  • Your restaurant's location.
  • Enticing images of the food and location.
  • Customer reviews and any awards received.

What features should a good restaurant website have?

Your website should include the basic information of menus, contact information, and reservation software, but also consider adding online ordering, photo galleries, and perhaps online ordering.

Should I have an FAQ page on my restaurant website?

An FAQ page is a useful resource to answer common customer queries, making it easier for them to find answers and engage with your brand.
