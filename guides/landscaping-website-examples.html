Landscaping Website Examples - 5 Sites That Grow Business

Detailed reviews of landscaping websites assessing functionality, visual appeal, UX, and content relevance.
By Carolin Hewitt, Jul 26, 2024
Carolin is a Copywriter for Zarla. She has a background in marketing communication and brand building, and has written articles about branding and logo design for small businesses.

As the online showroom of the business, a landscaping website should be visually appealing with ample, high-quality images of the business's handiwork, giving visitors a taste of the landscape transformations the company can do. Detailed descriptions demonstrating landscaping know-how and showcasing work methods can help make the sale.

To make it as easy as possible for potential customers to contact you, be sure to have prominent, compelling CTAs and one or more conspicuous contact options. With Zarla's landscaping AI website builder, getting started on your website is truly effortless, with a fully-fledged site delivered in under a minute.

How We Evaluate Websites:

Zarla checks that sites meet basic web standards for mobile and desktop. We also use Google's PageSpeed Insights, and WAVE, a service that evaluates accessibility for individuals with disabilities. PageSpeed scores and accessibility are important factors for search engine rankings. PageSpeed scores may not match user experience and can vary substantially.

Land Design Associates

https://www.landdesignassociates.com/

Boston, Massachusetts, U.S.

Land Design Associates is a landscaping company that serves the Greater Boston area, providing a host of services from landscape design and installation to maintenance and enhancements. At first glance, the website displays a well-organized layout that makes the comprehensive information provided on the website apparent without appearing overbearing.

Showcasing a few featured projects on the home page instantly provides a sense of the company's work. The value assigned to customer satisfaction is highlighted by the strong attention to reviews — more so than on competitors' sites. The video testimonials are especially engaging and establish trust.

Pros and Cons

The site is scattered with attractive, high-quality visuals and a few video clips, which are particularly compelling for customer reviews. The site's messaging is inviting and enticing, repeatedly reinforcing the concepts of dream gardens and the passion and expertise to create them.

Some images were slow to load when I accessed the site. The "Google Rating" link does not work properly, creating the false impression that the company does not have any reviews on Google. WAVE noted a number of low-contrast errors on the site, impeding legibility.

Desktop Review

The varying quality of the visual content seemed more noticeable to me on the desktop site, but aside from that, the browsing experience was very pleasant. Minor animated elements, such as graphics sliding into focus and images slowly enlarging, add a delightful liveliness.

While I experienced slow loading times, particularly in the "Projects" section, the site performed reasonably well on Google's PageSpeed Insights test, scoring 86/100 for performance.

Mobile Review

Some text on the mobile site is rather small, making it more straining to read. However, the prominent phone number in the hamburger menu stands out as a particularly helpful feature, although it could be highlighted in a different color to indicate that it is clickable. As with the desktop version, I experienced slow loading times, this time reflected in the lower performance score of 55/100 on Google's PageSpeed Insights.

Springhill Landscaping

https://www.springhilllandscapingllc.com/

Nampa, Idaho, U.S.

Springhill Landscaping is a landscaping and maintenance business for residential properties that also provides snow removal services. Their website uses a parallax scrolling effect, adding a sense of depth and making the browsing experience feel more immersive.

Client reviews are featured, establishing trust, although the average rating should be added alongside the "Overall Rating" heading. While the site has a common color scheme for this industry, it stood out to me because of its accessibility menu and neat, compact layout, enhancing the user experience.

Pros and Cons

The site features an accessibility menu and is easy to navigate, thanks to a simple but visually appealing layout. According to accessScan, the site is accessible and conforms with the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines international standard.

The site does not have a dedicated "About" page, provide insight into the company's history, or feature team profiles, making it feel rather impersonal. There is a notable repetition of keywords in the "Services" sections that should be omitted. The pages could also be consolidated, with more color variation and clickable boxes, for a more engaging user experience.

Desktop Review

The desktop site impressed as neat and professional, which I attribute to its clean, simple layout, concise top menu, and a good ratio of text to imagery. While I felt it had a strong visual appeal and was presented in a way that made information easy to digest, I missed elements that provide a more personal touch, such as staff photos and the company's story.

Mobile Review

While I thought that the mobile site could benefit from slight rearranging to break up longer sections of text with images, it was otherwise easy and enjoyable to peruse. The call icon at the top of the page is very convenient, and the accessibility menu icon is conspicuous. While the mobile site achieved perfect scores for SEO and best practices on Google's PageSpeed Insights, its performance score was 52/100.

M&M Gardening Services

https://mandmgardeningservices.co.uk/

Sheffield, England, U.K.

Established in 2007, M&M Gardening Services provides landscaping and gardening services to suit small and large budgets. Their website features a clean and well-spaced layout that could be made even more engaging with animated elements.

The "Reviews" page, while a good addition to build trust, looks particularly plain. However, I was delighted to discover a blog with useful tips and gardening inspiration, although it has been some time since the last article was added.

Pros and Cons

Contact information is easy to find, and there is a Google Maps integration, making it quick to locate the business. Reviews provide a trust-building element, and a good balance between text and imagery makes the site pleasant to browse.

The top of each page features a CTA and the business's phone number, but neither contains a link. accessScan's audit reported the site to be noncompliant with assistive technology, and WAVE flagged a multitude of low-contrast errors, making the site less accessible for users with visual difficulties.

Desktop Review

I thought the desktop site was clean and professional, and the pleasantly brief text sections accompanied by good-quality images made it easy for me to gain an overview of what is offered without overwhelming me with excessive details.

The desktop site achieved excellent scores for best practices and SEO on Google's PageSpeed Insights and had a good performance score of 85/100.

Mobile Review

Unlike the desktop site, the mobile version does not have a sticky menu, making navigation less intuitive. While the content resizes appropriately to fit smaller screen sizes, the spacing between content elements appears inconsistent and occasionally too large for my liking. The headline overlaying the hero image on the home page is not centered properly, making it feel amateurish.

Although the mobile site attained the same scores for best practices and SEO as the desktop site, its performance score on Google's PageSpeed Insights was lower at 66/100.

Landscape King

https://www.landscapekingla.com/

Baton Rouge, Louisiana, U.S.

Landscape King offers residential and commercial landscaping services and highlights a passion for the job and commitment to customer satisfaction in the company website's "About Us" section. Instead of using a green-only color scheme, which is common in this industry, Landscape King's branding features bright blue, adding more zest to the brand.

The website is comprehensive in terms of content; details about the company's process, services, story, and projects are easy to find. The landscaping videos provide helpful information while highlighting the personality and approach of the team.

Pros and Cons

Visitors are presented with detailed information about the business and its services, and images of the team add a personal touch, making the business approachable. Prominent CTA buttons make it easy for visitors to contact the company, and reviews add a trust-building element.

The site is not fully compliant with assistive technology, according to accessScan, and WAVE flagged some low-contrast errors and instances of images missing alternative text. The alignment of paragraphs is not consistent on all pages and makes the site look less polished.

Desktop Review

While I found the desktop website very engaging, thanks to ample imagery, its lively color palette, and plenty of CTA buttons, the extensive copy on some pages felt a little overwhelming. However, this was less noticeable on a larger desktop screen, with the floating menu providing easy navigation between dense pages. The desktop site scored well in the performance and best practices categories on Google's PageSpeed Insights at 95/100 and 96/100, respectively.

Mobile Review

I was pleasantly surprised by the mobile version, which maintained the rich visual elements of the desktop site that are often reduced on mobile counterparts. While they look great, the downside is a decrease in loading speeds.

The lengthier text sections that already appear a little overpowering on the desktop site would benefit from being collapsed with a "Read more" button, allowing users to expand the text on the mobile site to avoid a more intense effect. Unlike the desktop site, which had a very good performance score on Google's PageSpeed Insights, the mobile site scored a lower 49/100.

Modern Landscape Creations

https://www.modernlandscapecreations.com.au/

Sydney, New South Wales, Australia

Modern Landscape Creations is a landscaping company that proclaims a passion for creating beautiful, creative, and custom outdoor living spaces. Their website's black and gold color scheme, unusual for a landscaping business, made it stand out to me instantly. Overall, the site maintains a simple elegance with its sleek design and succinct text, although some sections could be elaborated on, such as the "Services" page, which could do with individual service descriptions.

While featuring recent projects on the home page provides a glance at the company's work, I thought the before-and-after image sliders included on some of the project-specific pages and review section added a particularly fun and engaging way to explore transformations.

Pros and Cons

A friendly photo of the team adds a personal touch to the otherwise sophisticated, minimalist appeal of the site. Reviews support credibility and build trust, and recent projects are showcased with detailed descriptions to convey the company's skills and capabilities. Though the link is rather inconspicuous, the FAQ section provides salient information at a glance.

While the images on the "Services" page are clickable, they link to this same "Services" page instead of a separate section with more detail about the specific service. WAVE flagged dozens of errors across the site, indicating reduced accessibility, and according to accessScan, the site is not fully compliant with assistive technology.

Desktop Review

The visual content grabbed my attention on the desktop site, with minor animated features adding an engaging element. I found the autoplay feature on the "Reviews" page somewhat irritating, since sound is enabled, but had an otherwise enjoyable browsing experience. While the desktop site scored an excellent 100/100 for best practices, it had a slightly lower performance score of 89/100 on Google's PageSpeed Insights.

Mobile Review

The hamburger menu on the mobile site does not stand out very well against the dark background, but when I opened it to reveal a drop-down menu, the items stood out crisp and clear. While media content and text resize appropriately on most pages, the "Philosophy" section on the home page should be collapsed to a preview snippet with a "Read more" button for a more streamlined user experience.

On Google's PageSpeed Insights test, the mobile site scored 96/100 for best practices, a touch lower than the desktop version, and its performance score was 61/100.
