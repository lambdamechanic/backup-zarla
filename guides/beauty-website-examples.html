Beauty Website Examples - 10 Stellar Sites That Capture Customers

Discover what makes an effective beauty website and be inspired by these 10 examples.
By Lee-Shay Newton, Jul 25, 2024
Lee-Shay Newton is an Editor and Copywriter at Zarla. She has written for various print and online publications and enjoys creating engaging content on website design.

An exceptional beauty website should combine aesthetics, functionality, and personalized elements to create a great user experience. Comprehensive product ranges, interactive features, expert advice, and transparency are key to creating an exemplary site. How We Evaluate Websites:

Zarla checks that sites meet basic web standards for mobile and desktop. We also use Google's PageSpeed Insights, and WAVE, a service that evaluates accessibility for individuals with disabilities. PageSpeed scores and accessibility are important factors for search engine rankings. PageSpeed scores may not match user experience and can vary substantially.

Sephora

https://www.sephora.com/

Neuilly-sur-Seine, France

Sephora is a world-renowned beauty retailer founded by Dominique Mandonnaud in France in 1979. The brand is known for combining classic beauty brands with new and inspiring lines and aims to provide customers with trusted, high-quality products that are easy to use and accessible to all.

The Sephora website has a clean and intuitive interface, with visually appealing elements, detailed product descriptions, user reviews, and filtering options. Notable features include the Beauty Insider Community and online chat support with beauty representatives.

Pros and Cons

Sephora has a user-friendly desktop site and features various elements to make the shopping experience convenient for customers, such as a store and services selector, personalized recommendations, and free standard shipping.

We experienced slow loading times on certain pages and felt the mobile site was not as intuitive as the desktop version. WAVE detected missing alternative text and empty links, which could impact users with visual impairments.

Desktop Review

Sephora's intuitive menu allows visitors to browse by product type, concern, bestsellers, and skincare regimen, creating a convenient browsing experience. At the top of the home page, the "Community" and "Stores & Services" features encourage users to engage with the site and brand. While the site received a great SEO rating, it scored 40/100 for performance on Google's PageSpeed Insights test.

Mobile Review

On mobile, the layout of the home page is not as easy to navigate as the desktop site, with users having to scroll horizontally to see the various elements. In addition, the intuitive menu has been replaced by a single line of product categories, whereas a hamburger or drop-down menu would have ensured better navigation. The site scored 32/100 for performance on PageSpeed Insights.

Tester Korea

https://testerkorea.com/

Goyang-si, Gyeonggi-do, South Korea

Tester Korea is a K-beauty website founded in 2007. The site offers a variety of skincare, makeup, and bath and body products. However, its key drawcard is the sale of testers, allowing customers to try out different brands before committing to full-size products.

The website features a clean aesthetic with vibrant imagery and colors serving to attract a young demographic. Aside from individual categories, the site has dedicated pages for testers, promotions, special sets, and brands. In addition, links to the account section, shipping information, and customer support can be easily found.

Pros and Cons

The site is well organized, with links to sets, promotions, brands, and samples prominently displayed in the menu bar. The site also has a French Canadian version.

On desktop, only product images are hyperlinked, meaning that users can't click on the product text or price to access individual product pages. Certain sections of the site are only accessible to users with an account. The site lacks customer reviews and a guest checkout option. WAVE detected multiple accessibility errors.

Desktop Review

The home page loads quickly but there is some lag on the product pages. Each product features a detailed description with how-to instructions; however, the layout of the text is awkwardly centered which affects the aesthetics of the page.

The sticky menu remains fixed when scrolling and there's a tab on the right featuring a back-to-top button, recent cart additions, and links to the customer inquiry and account pages. However, this tab can only be seen in full when you zoom out a little, which can be annoying for users. The site performed well on the PageSpeed Insights test, receiving 88/100.

Mobile Review

The site adapts well to mobile screens and images appear crisper. In contrast to the desktop site, the entire product block is selectable. The menu is also sticky and users can easily access their accounts, shopping cart, and customer support via the top menu or footer links.

In addition, the search bar is larger and more prominent than on desktop. However, the back-to-top tab from the desktop site has disappeared and users have to scroll to the top of the screen manually. While the site scored 28/100 for performance on PageSpeed Insights, it fell well within acceptable ranges in our real-life tests.

Ilia Beauty

https://iliabeauty.com/

Laguna Beach, California, U.S.

Ilia Beauty is a cruelty-free beauty and skincare brand founded by Sasha Plavsic in 2011. The brand is committed to providing clean, skin-centric products created with safe yet potent formulas and active skincare ingredients.

The website presents a clean and modern aesthetic that aligns with the brand's values and achieves this through visually appealing imagery, intuitive navigation, informative product pages, and a streamlined shopping experience. A photo of Plavsic, along with her founding story on the About page, adds a personal touch.

Pros and Cons

The site features interactive elements and high-quality images to enhance the user experience. There are multiple currency options for customers in the U.S., Canada, and the U.K. The Help Center provides clear information on product ingredients, shipping options, and order tracking. The home page features cruelty-free and environmental badges, establishing trust.

Makeup tutorial videos do not appear consistently throughout the site. WAVE reported multiple low-contrast errors, which may affect visually impaired users.

Desktop Review

The home page presents an organized layout with video, images, and products in a grid style, allowing for a seamless experience on desktop. The product range is easily accessible at the top of the home page, and various call-to-action buttons encourage visitors to shop specific products or collections.

The product pages load quickly and all information is displayed in a comprehensive and easy-to-digest way. However, the site could benefit from a back-to-top button given the amount of information present on each page. On Google's PageSpeed Insights test, the site scored 43/100 for performance.

Mobile Review

The site scales well on smaller screens, and images and videos load fairly quickly despite the site's performance score of 36/100 on Google's PageSpeed Insights test.

The menu has been converted to three tabs at the top of the screen, allowing easy access to bestsellers, sets, and the "Find My Shade" tool, while additional product page links are found via the hamburger menu. In contrast to the desktop site, some of the product text may be difficult for some to read due to the smaller font size. The mobile site also lacks a back-to-top button.

Maybelline

https://www.maybelline.com/

Manhattan, New York City, U.S.

Maybelline is one of the oldest beauty brands in the U.S. and is found in over 120 cities worldwide. The Maybelline website portrays the brand's energy and trendsetting nature with a striking color palette, attractive imagery, and bold typography to grab visitors' attention.

The site features intuitive navigation and various interactive elements that are unique to the brand. Moreover, helpful elements such as accessibility features, a location selector, and quizzes, aim to create a user-friendly experience. However, the site does not have online chat support or offer direct shipping.

Pros and Cons

The site features unique interactive elements, such as virtual try-on tools, image sliders, and quizzes, to enhance the user experience. An accessibility feature allows visitors to change the contrast of the site and switch off animations.

Maybelline directs visitors to other retailer sites to purchase products, as they do not offer direct sales, and prices are only visible on individual product pages. The amount of media on the site causes an overcrowded effect, and contact details are not easy to find. WAVE detected a number of contrast errors and empty links.

Desktop Review

Clicking through the menu on the home page opens up a submenu, and the user must make a selection to continue to the product page they are interested in. The menu is not sticky when scrolling down, but appears when scrolling up.

Each product page is highly detailed, with descriptions, dynamic images, and interactive tools, allowing visitors to see what the makeup looks like on a person. While these are helpful for the user, the large amount of media on each page can be overwhelming. On PageSpeed Insights, the site received a mid-range performance score of 68/100.

Mobile Review

The home page does not adapt well to smaller screens, with users having to scroll horizontally to see various sections of the page. In contrast to the desktop site, however, the menu is easier to navigate and floats when scrolling up and down. The images are also sharper and pages load faster.

Furthermore, the store selector and accessibility feature have moved to the bottom of the opened hamburger menu, and the search function is more prominent, as are the "Mood Board" and quiz pop-ups at the bottom right of the screen. The site scored 32/100 for performance on PageSpeed Insights.

Green People

https://www.greenpeople.co.uk/

West Grinstead, England, U.K.

Green People is an award-winning organic skincare brand founded by Charlotte Vøhtz in 1997. The lack of gentle natural products at the time led her to create her own line of organic creams for her daughter's allergy-prone skin. Since then, the company has been committed to preserving the environment and providing effective and safe organic products for all.

The Green People site effectively showcases the brand's product range with an organized menu, informative product pages, and a responsive design. Notable features include a skincare quiz, rewards program, and special offers section.

Pros and Cons

The site offers clear product categories, online chat support, and various resources to aid users. According to WAVE, the site has almost no accessibility issues.

The site's product images do not include dynamic shots of makeup looks or skincare routines commonly found on other beauty sites.

Desktop Review

The desktop site offers a comprehensive and user-friendly browsing experience with an organized menu, allowing visitors to shop by concern, product, interest, and solution. The product pages are highly detailed, with information on benefits for the skin, product ingredients, and instructions on how to use the products and recycle packaging.

Visitors can also access customer reviews and ratings to gauge the performance and efficacy of the products before purchasing them. The site performed well on Google's PageSpeed Insights test, scoring 66/100.

Mobile Review

On mobile, the menu and categories are prominent and easy to navigate, and product pages load fast and are easy to filter and sort. The "Special Offers" tab on the right of the screen is more prominent than on desktop, prompting users to peruse the current specials.

It's quick and easy to add items to cart, with various payment options and express checkout available for users who are hesitant to create an account. On Google's PageSpeed Insights test, the site scored 62/100 for performance.

ActiveSkin

https://www.activeskin.com.au/

Balgowlah, New South Wales, Australia

ActiveSkin is a premium online destination and stockist of over 150 skincare, makeup, wellness, and hair care brands. The brand was founded by Yvette Wyeth in 1999 and became an online store in 2006.

ActiveSkin stands out for its vast array of products, and the site is run by a team of skincare and beauty professionals, ensuring that customers receive expert advice and knowledge on which products to use. Blog posts, value bundles, and various support options serve to attract a wide clientele.

Pros and Cons

The site features a minimalistic design, intuitive navigation, and a streamlined checkout process. Same-day shipping and free delivery options are available. WAVE detected very few errors.

Images take longer to load on the mobile site. The extensive menu may seem overwhelming on desktop, and there are no video tutorials to aid users.

Desktop Review

The desktop site's key features are its intuitive menu and organized product range, which can be filtered and sorted according to the user's needs. Visitors can also shop by product type, skincare concern, and ingredients, adding an additional level of efficiency to the site.

The shopping process is quick and easy, with multiple online payment options available. The site performed well overall on Google's PageSpeed Insights test, receiving a mid-range score of 57/100 for performance.

Mobile Review

While the images take longer to load on mobile, the text is easier to read than on desktop and the menu is easier to digest. However, some of the elements on the home page can only be viewed when scrolling horizontally, which can be tedious for users. The mobile site scored 33/100 on PageSpeed Insights.

Top Secret Studio

https://topsecret.com.sg/

Midview City, Singapore

Top Secret Studio started as a shopping platform for haircare products but later expanded its range to include skincare and bath and body products. The site aims to provide customers with international brands at competitive prices, as well as a convenient shopping experience and excellent customer service.

The website boasts a clean and modern aesthetic with an organized layout and clear product categories. An intuitive menu allows visitors to easily find what they're looking for, and the shopping process is quick and easy.

Pros and Cons

The site offers intuitive navigation and a streamlined shopping process with secure payment options. There's a convenient mobile app for Android and iOS users.

There is no information about the founder or any reviews from customers who've purchased products from the site. WAVE detected several missing alternative text and low-contrast errors.

Desktop Review

The home page features a large image carousel, which instantly draws attention and adds visual appeal to the otherwise stark aesthetic. A notable element is the "Featured Categories" section, which contains cute line-drawn images of women using beauty products.

The sticky menu bar is easy to navigate, with clear categories and the ability to select hair care products according to individual hair concerns. The menu also links to a store location page featuring the address and contact information of the brand's physical stores. On PageSpeed Insights, the site scored 75/100 for performance.

Mobile Review

As with the desktop site, the intuitive menu is easy to navigate, and the shopping process is seamless. The menu bar floats as you scroll and it is easy to search for products, add items to a "favorites" list, or access the shopping cart via the icons at the top right.

Users also have quick access to the "About Us" section and location and contact information in the footer. The site scored 46/100 for performance on PageSpeed Insights.

Lola Lee

https://www.lolaleebeauty.com/

Umhlanga, KwaZulu Natal, South Africa

Lola Lee is a gel polish brand designed by industry professionals in South Africa. The brand prides itself on its range of 100% vegan nail polishes, promising customers longevity, quality, and style in a range of vibrant colors based on seasonal beauty trends. The brand is recognized by Beauty Without Cruelty.

The brand's website showcases its ethos of cruelty-free beauty and its extensive range of nail polish products in an elegant, nonmoralizing way. It also provides information on training courses, videos, and product guides.

Pros and Cons

The site has interactive images, a visually appealing layout, and easy navigation. The home page features links and badges reinforcing its cruelty-free, vegan-friendly stance on beauty. Free shipping is offered for orders above a minimum spend.

There is no information about the founder or any customer reviews on the site. WAVE detected over 20 errors, including empty links and buttons, and missing alternative text.

Desktop Review

The site features a traditionally feminine aesthetic with high-quality images of products and tools neatly displayed in a grid style, which creates an aesthetically pleasing experience for visitors.

Interactive elements, varied images of the products, and detailed product and application descriptions all serve to enhance the user experience. Moreover, the shopping process is seamless and contact information is easy to find. The site scored 90/100 for performance on PageSpeed Insights.

Mobile Review

The fast loading times are not carried through to the mobile site, with most images and product pages taking a long time to load. Access to the customer account page, shopping cart, and menu is easily available at the top of the screen, and various secure payment options are available at checkout. The site scored 72/100 for performance on Google's PageSpeed Insights.

Cosmetify

https://www.cosmetify.com/

London, England, U.K.

Cosmetify is a beauty shopping platform that features makeup, skincare, wellness, and fragrance products from over 1,700 brands. The site features a clean, modern design with a simple menu and plenty of white space for a pleasing browser experience.

Users can easily switch between the U.S. and U.K. sites, and there are dedicated pages for sale items and blog posts to help visitors make informed decisions. A TrustPilot logo pictured in the header with an "Excellent" rating adds credibility to the site.

Pros and Cons

The site offers a user-friendly browsing experience, with a simple checkout process, and various payment and shipping options. Social media links are easy to find and a mobile app is available for Android and iOS users. WAVE picked up very few errors.

The search bar does not function on the mobile site. Many pages do not feature full ingredient lists, which may hinder users with sensitive skincare needs.

Desktop Review

The neatly laid-out home page features a minimized menu that opens up a wider selection of categories, making navigation a breeze. However, the search bar is unnecessarily large, squeezing the menu into a smaller space at the top-right.

The product pages feature extensive filter and sorting options, allowing users to find what they need quickly, while individual product pages have a good balance of text and images. However, the site could benefit from a sticky menu and back-to-top button. Overall, the site garnered top PageSpeed Insights scores, receiving 96/100 for performance on desktop.

Mobile Review

The home page does not resize appropriately for mobile screens, with users having to scroll horizontally on various sections of the page. However, the intuitive menu is as easy to navigate as it is on desktop and floats when scrolling, contrary to the desktop site.

While shopping, the "Add to Bag" button pops up at the bottom of the screen when scrolling, and users can easily access their shopping cart and account page at all times. The mobile site performed equally well on PageSpeed Insights, receiving a rating of 86/100 for performance.

Nature Republic

https://nrirvine.com/

Irvine, California, U.S.

Nature Republic is a K-beauty skincare line founded in Southern California in 2019. The brand has retail stores across 20 countries, with a flagship store in Irvine. Nature Republic's philosophy is that healthy skincare, enriched with natural ingredients, should be accessible and affordable to all.

The website features a neat layout with a clean, minimalistic aesthetic that appeals to a discerning clientele who take their beauty regimens seriously. A free shipping option with a minimum spend and various offers, rewards, and free gifts serve to attract visitors' attention.

Pros and Cons

Nature Republic offers a fast checkout process, and numerous offers and resources to attract customers. "Ask A Question" forms on product pages encourage interaction.

The website suffers from slow loading times on certain pages. Not all product pages feature ingredient lists. WAVE reported a few empty links and missing alt text.

Desktop Review

The desktop site is easy to navigate, with product categories easily accessible from the menu bar and center of the home page. The brand's "About Us" section, location, opening hours, and contact details are easy to find.

Comprehensive filter and sorting options are present on all product pages, and varied images of each product are helpful. In addition, the shopping process is easy and straightforward. The site received a performance score of 33/100 on PageSpeed Insights.

Mobile Review

As with the desktop site, the product pages tend to load slowly on mobile devices. However, the site's simplistic theme and easy navigation are continued throughout the platform, allowing customers to view and purchase products easily. The site scored 44/100 for performance on PageSpeed Insights.

What is the best website to sell beauty products?

The best website to sell beauty products varies depending on your specific needs and goals. However, ecommerce capabilities, intuitive navigation, and customization tools are important elements of a well-functioning beauty website. Take a look at the collection of beauty website examples we've curated for inspiration.

What should I put on a beauty website?

  • High-resolution images of your products and/or services.
  • Informative product descriptions.
  • Attractive branding.
  • Clear product categories.
  • Engaging content, such as blog posts.
  • An online store with a secure checkout process.
  • Responsive design elements.
  • Social media integration.
  • Customer testimonials.
  • Contact information.

What is the purpose of a beauty website?

The purpose of a beauty website is multifaceted: it allows you to showcase your products or services to customers, facilitate sales if your goal is to sell products online, and promote your brand identity and ethos to the public. It also allows you to attract new clients and gather feedback about what your customers liked or didn't like about your offerings via product reviews.
