Musician Website Examples - 5 Sites That Connect With Audiences

These 5 amazing musician website examples make a statement with impactful images and easy-to-navigate content.
By Carla Lottering, Jul 26, 2024
Carla is a Copywriter for Zarla based in Cape Town, South Africa. She has over 5 years' experience in online content creation and writes extensively on logos for small businesses.

Musicians put a lot of time and effort into their craft and, as such, their websites should reflect their unique sound and convey ideas of joy, charisma, and passion with high-quality images and SEO-friendly content. It should also feature easily accessible information regarding live performances, merchandise purchases, and booking processes.

How We Evaluate Websites:

Zarla checks that sites meet basic web standards for mobile and desktop. We also use Google's PageSpeed Insights, and WAVE, a service that evaluates accessibility for individuals with disabilities. PageSpeed scores and accessibility are important factors for search engine rankings. PageSpeed scores may not match user experience and can vary substantially.

Jazmine Sullivan

https://www.jazminesullivanmusic.com/

Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, U.S.

Jazmine Sullivan is a Grammy-winning R&B and soul singer-songwriter, recently placed on TIME's 100 Most Influential People list. The website has a funky yet contemporary appearance with blocky typography and a striking color palette. The stylish hero image prominently displays the artist's aesthetic and denotes a feeling of power.

The site is a good visual representation of the artist and allows visitors to get to know their music with attention-grabbing images and videos, albeit a unique personal touch is missing, which can be improved with an "About" section or introductory video.

Pros and Cons

The website performs well on WAVE's accessibility test with few contrast errors. Some pertinent information, such as tour dates and music videos, can be found on the home page in well-spaced and engaging page sections.

Unlike some competitors' sites, the music videos do not play on the website but rather redirect to the artist's YouTube channel, hindering site navigation slightly. The lack of a sticky top menu bar is noticeable, as is the absence of an "About" section.

Desktop Review

The desktop version has an efficient design that effectively highlights its intuitive navigation options. However, some page elements, such as the tour schedule, take a long time to load and the top menu bar disappears into the hero slideshow transitions, which impacts the site's usability. On Google's PageSpeed Insights test, the website scored 95/100 in the performance category.

Mobile Review

The mobile site does a better job of displaying the artist's social media links but is also missing a sticky top menu bar. Its videos and images display nicely without losing functionality and feature a responsive design that's ideal for smaller screens. Contrary to the desktop version, the site's performance score on Google's PageSpeed Insights test is on the lower end of the spectrum at 48/100.

Rush

https://www.rush.com/

Toronto, Ontario, Canada

Rush is an iconic band founded in the early 1970s with a progressive rock sound. The website has a dark color scheme that, in unison with the modern typeface and good selection of high-resolution images, creates a concert-inspired design that effectively advertises the band's latest news, discography, and online shop.

However, the site should expand its content selection beyond news updates and touring archives to enhance its overall appeal. It should also optimize its shopping experience to attract more customers.

Pros and Cons

The band's logo is prominently displayed and effectively conveys brand cohesion. The search bar is a helpful addition for visitors in search of specific information. According to WAVE, the website has no contrast errors, indicating optimal readability.

Beyond basic navigation, the website does not offer additional features like music or live concert videos to entice visitors to explore the band's musical offerings. The "Shop" page does not display properly and may deter potential customers from making a purchase.

Desktop Review

The desktop version functions well with fast page loading times, and it received a good performance score of 93/100 on Google's PageSpeed Insights test, but there is room for improvement in the SEO practices category. However, the shopping experience on desktop is not intuitive and users are better served purchasing the band's merchandise on the mobile version.

Mobile Review

The mobile website features clean page layouts and good navigation options with a search bar and sticky hamburger menu. The online shop also displays correctly and offers a much better user experience than its desktop counterpart. On Google's PageSpeed Insights test, the website scored 73/100 in the performance category, and it also indicates reasonable page loading times.

Nile

https://nile-official.com/

Greenville, South Carolina, U.S.

Nile is a death metal band that incorporates Egyptian and Lovecraftian themes into their music. The website's aesthetic remains consistent with the band's genre, creating a moody appearance with the use of ancient symbolism across the site. The website's content-rich pages allow users to easily explore the band's music, tour dates, and merchandise.

Pros and Cons

The home page features a comprehensive overview of the band's touring information with vibrant posters and tour images, set against a background of fans enjoying their concert, creating an eye-catching appearance. The "About" page features personable content and images that effectively contrast the site's otherwise aggressive design elements.

On WAVE, the website has several contrast errors that indicate insufficient contrast between the home page's hero image and the top menu bar. The abundance of images and videos may slow down page loading times on some devices.

Desktop Review

The desktop version effectively conveys the band's hardcore appeal while ensuring a pleasant user experience with easy-to-navigate pages, informative content, and an interactive Spotify playlist. However, some key elements, such as a sticky top menu bar and comprehensive footer menu, are missing. On Google's PageSpeed Insights test, the desktop site scored 99/100 in the performance category.

Mobile Review

The mobile website retains a dark aesthetic and presents as user-friendly with easy site navigation and large social media links for optimal user engagement. However, the text on the posters displaying the band's tour dates is quite small and difficult to read. As with the desktop site, the lack of a comprehensive footer menu is noticeable. The website's performance scored 70/100 on Google's PageSpeed Insights test, which indicates fast page loading times.

Joan Osborne

https://joanosborne.com/

Anchorage, Kentucky, U.S.

Joan Osborne is a multi-platinum singer, songwriter, and painter specializing in rock, pop, soul, blues, and country music. The website showcases the singer's personal brand effectively with an impactful hero image slideshow, high-quality music videos, and minimal color usage that captures their artistic sensibilities. The site's online store presents a seamless shopping experience with an easy online payment option for both local and global customers.

Pros and Cons

The website is content-rich with pertinent information available on the website, and additional links to the singer's biography, merchandise store, and contact options listed in the floating top menu bar. The singer's discography, listed in Spotify and Apple Music playlist formats on the home page, are helpful additions that allow users to explore the artist's music.

The top menu bar does not sufficiently stand out against the hero image and only becomes more readable as you scroll down. A large number of contrast errors were picked up by WAVE, indicating suboptimal readability across the site.

Desktop Review

The desktop site's larger screen allows the artist's images, music videos, and clean page layouts to shine. However, the use of more prominent CTA buttons may encourage increased user engagement. According to Google's PageSpeed Insights test, there is room for improvement with the site's accessibility and SEO practices, which are scored 81/100 and 85/100 respectively.

Mobile Review

The mobile site slims down the images without losing quality, ensuring a good user experience. Unlike the desktop version, all content stands out effectively against the white background with no significant readability issues. While real-world experience indicates reasonable page loading times, the mobile site's performance score on Google's PageSpeed Insights test is lower than average at 42/100.

Infinity Song

https://infinitysongmusic.com/

New York City, New York, U.S.

Infinity Song is a sibling band that creates soft rock and RnB music from their home base of New York City. The website strikes a good balance between simplicity and artistic expression with minimalist design elements and informative music-related content.

Visitors can easily navigate the site to find information about their music stylings, upcoming performances, and merchandise. The website's online store has several payment options, ensuring a seamless shopping experience.

Pros and Cons

The band's biography conveys their ethos of family and the importance of a musical upbringing, feeling personable and informative. The use of high-quality images and neat typography emphasizes the band's clean aesthetic and ensures visitors can focus on the site's essential content, such as their tour dates and music videos.

WAVE picked up several contrast errors on the website, particularly between the red top menu bar text and the hero image. The search icon in the top-left corner is a helpful addition but hardly visible, hindering its purpose. The same can be said for the social media links in the top-right corner.

Desktop Review

The desktop website has an impactful visual appeal with high-resolution images and videos, as well as clean page layouts. However, important site navigation tools, like a sticky top menu bar and robust footer menu, are missing. On Google's PageSpeed Insights test, the site's accessibility and SEO practices are well-scored at 90/100 and 92/100 respectively.

Mobile Review

The mobile website's hero image is much smaller and blends a little into the social media links, negating some of the visual impact experienced on the desktop version. The easy-to-read content presents no real usability issues, but page loading times are quite slow, indicated by the site's performance score of 42/100 on Google's PageSpeed Insights test.
