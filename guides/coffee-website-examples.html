Coffee Website Examples - 5 In-Depth Reviews of Sites That Brew Up a Great User Experience

Create a memorable online presence for your business with our review of 5 coffee website examples.
By Carla Lottering, Jul 25, 2024
Carla is a Copywriter for Zarla based in Cape Town, South Africa. She has over 5 years' experience in online content creation and writes extensively on logos for small businesses.

The coffee industry is a competitive market that attracts bean aficionados in search of the perfect brew. As such, a coffee website should present readers with an engaging online experience through the use of high-resolution images advertising its product range, along with simple navigation options.

Other factors that contribute to an unforgettable coffee website include product pricing and availability, as well as the business location so readers can easily visit the roastery or coffee shop. Use our reviews as guidance when you create your own website with our coffee AI website builder.

How We Evaluate Websites:

Zarla checks that sites meet basic web standards for mobile and desktop. We also use Google's PageSpeed Insights, and WAVE, a service that evaluates accessibility for individuals with disabilities. PageSpeed scores and accessibility are important factors for search engine rankings. PageSpeed scores may not match user experience and can vary substantially.

Extract Coffee Roasters

https://extractcoffee.co.uk/

Bristol, England, U.K.

Extract Coffee Roasters specializes in roasting ethically sourced, single-origin arabica coffee for locally-based clients. The website blends soft color accents with whimsical imagery and typography to create a stylish appearance that effectively advertises the company's range of coffee products. The business's sustainability ethos is prominently displayed on the home page and will appeal to environmentally conscious coffee lovers.

Visitors benefit from ample page links that feature narrative-rich content on the company's history and team members, as well as its online shop with subscription options, gift cards, and brewing equipment.

Pros and Cons

The brand's special offers and discounts are prominently advertised on the home page, encouraging user engagement. A standout feature is the "Ask the Prof" section, which helps users find their perfect coffee match with a quiz. WAVE picked up only a few contrast errors across the website.

The site would benefit from displaying its contact options more prominently to ensure users can easily get in touch with the company. According to accessScan, the website is only semi-compliant with assistive technologies.

Desktop Review

The desktop website's clean and refined design elements, including fun graphics and high-quality images, are displayed effectively, and the various content sections are well-spaced for easy reading. However, some of the site's pertinent information, such as its contact details and FAQ section, is only accessible in the footer menu, which may be more useful if moved to the top menu bar.

On Google's PageSpeed Insights test, the website has a good performance score of 91/100, but the test also indicated that its accessibility practices can be improved upon.

Mobile Review

While the mobile site performs adequately with good page loading times, its performance score on Google's PageSpeed Insights test is average at 58/100. The various content sections slim down nicely to fit the smaller screen without losing functionality, and all images remain high-quality, although the hero image gets cut off.

Unlike the desktop version, the mobile site's hamburger menu hides some pertinent navigation options, such as the search bar and shopping bag, which would be better used if added to the top menu bar.

Silver Bridge Coffee Company

https://silverbridgecoffee.com/

Bidwell, Ohio, U.S.

Silver Bridge Coffee Company is a women-owned business that sources and roasts specialty coffee beans. The website effectively utilizes the company's 20s-inspired logo to create a stylish design that's complemented by muted color accents. Visitors gain access to the company's wide variety of coffee products, as well as an informative coffee blog.

The company's commitment to community upliftment is prominently displayed on its "Giving Back" page, and the company also works with other businesses to create FUNdraising programs, which help them raise funds through the sale of its products.

Pros and Cons

The home page's introductory video depicts the company's staff members and work processes, giving visitors a good overview of their industry and product expertise. The online shopping experience is seamless, and users receive free shipping on certain orders, which encourages increased user engagement.

Some of the larger images on the site's "About" page are pixelated and could be displayed in a smaller size for a better visual impact. According to WAVE, the site has a large number of contrast errors.

Desktop Review

The desktop website's content is easily readable but can be slightly overwhelming on some pages, which can be improved upon with additional images to break up text-heavy sections. The back-to-top button contributes to a smooth user experience, although some interactive elements, including the "Our Story" video, are faulty and distract from the site's appeal.

Mobile Review

On the mobile version, the company's logo is displayed in the top menu bar and does not have the same visual impact as on desktop. The "Our Story" page's video also does not play correctly on mobile, but the site does include a helpful search bar, which is not available on desktop. According to Google's PageSpeed Insights test, the website has a higher-than-average performance score of 93/100.

Camellia Coffee Roasters

https://camelliacoffeeroasters.com/

Sacramento, California, U.S.

Camellia Coffee Roasters is a coffee shop and roastery that offers a comprehensive menu of coffee beans, beverages, and accompanying breakfast and lunch food items. The website features whimsical graphics that personalize the brand's wide product range, and its elegant color accents allow the informative content, including its company history and roasting methodology, to stand out.

The website offers both retail and wholesale options with partnership programs for other businesses interested in selling the company's coffee products.

Pros and Cons

The site's "About" section is informative and includes an audio pronunciation of the company's name, which is a fun and helpful addition. The online shopping and ordering experience is intuitive, and WAVE picked up only a few contrast errors across the site.

The website would benefit from additional resources, such as blog posts and an FAQ section, to round out its content offerings. According to Google's PageSpeed Insights test, the site's pages load slowly on both desktop and mobile.

Desktop Review

The desktop website is both visually appealing and easy to navigate, encouraging visitors to learn more about the company and its products with clear CTA buttons. Thanks to the ample white space and large headings, the content is easily readable, although the search bar and shopping bag icons in the top-right corner could be more prominently displayed. On Google's PageSpeed Insights test, the site's performance scored 82/100.

Mobile Review

The mobile version performs adequately and slims down the images and content without losing functionality. However, the "Shop" CTA button gets hidden behind the hamburger menu, which seems like a missed advertising opportunity. According to Google's PageSpeed Insights test, the site's pages are slow to load, but real-world experience indicates a more pleasant user experience. The test also gave the website a performance score of 32/100.

Lost Aviator Coffee Co.

https://www.lostaviatorcoffee.com/

Guelph, Ontario, Canada

Lost Aviator Coffee Co. is a coffee roaster with a locally-based coffee bar that offers a wide range of products, including signature blends and single-origin coffees. The website features Aviation-inspired imagery that ties in nicely with the company name, while the gray-scale color palette creates a sophisticated and professional appeal.

The site's ample page links include narrative-rich content on the company's coffee products and merchandise, as well as its subscription service for coffee connoisseurs and an informative blog that features coffee recipes and brewing tips.

Pros and Cons

The site's "Reviews" tab features a large selection of client testimonials, which adds credibility. The "Best Selling Coffee" product category gives new clients a good overview of the company's most popular coffees.

The site would benefit from additional CTA buttons to increase user engagement. WAVE picked up several contrast errors on the website, and the footer menu's social media links are quite small and easy to miss.

Desktop Review

The desktop website has a good performance score of 92/100 on Google's PageSpeed Insights test, but it also indicated room for improvement with its accessibility and SEO practices. Readers can easily find pertinent information, such as contact details, from the site's top menu bar, and the addition of a search bar icon is helpful for smooth browsing.

Mobile Review

The mobile website gets rid of some extra white space and condenses the content and images nicely to optimize readability. Unlike the desktop version, the mobile site has a back-to-top button, which contributes to an enhanced user experience. Contrary to real-world experience, Google's PageSpeed Insights test indicates slow page loading times and a performance score of 35/100.

Merchant Coffee Roasters

http://merchantcoffeeroasters.com.au/

Mansfield, Victoria, Australia

Merchant Coffee Roasters is a coffee roastery and café that sells a wide range of specialty coffee beans and beverages, as well as subscription boxes. The website blends a dark color scheme with bright images to create a visually appealing design that effectively conveys the company's affinity for creativity.

The site's clear page categorizations ensure visitors can easily find out more about the company's roastery, explore its product range, and apply for one of its barista courses. A notable feature is its top menu bar with important contact information listed for easy access to the team.

Pros and Cons

The site's "Shop" page includes special promotions and discounts to entice users to make a purchase, while the café's menu is accessible for those visiting the roastery. WAVE picked up only a few instances of insufficient contrast ratios.

The website would benefit from additional resources like the brand's roasting process or a blog posts section to give users a more holistic overview of the company. The website does not possess a SSL certificate for additional security.

Desktop Review

The desktop version has a functional layout and a comprehensive selection of page links to ensure users have access to vital information. However, the top menu bar text could be sized up slightly for easier readability. While real-world experience indicates a user-friendly experience, Google's PageSpeed Insights test shows slow page loading times on desktop. Its performance score, however, is quite high at 95/100.

Mobile Review

The mobile site has a visually engaging home page with a refined hero image and large search bar, as well as pertinent contact information listed above the top menu bar instead of hidden behind the hamburger menu. On Google's PageSpeed Insights test, the website scored 62/100 in the performance category. Slow page loading times were also noted on the test, but real-world experience indicates a more intuitive experience.

What is the purpose of a coffee website?

A coffee website acts as an online showroom and shop for visitors to easily find and explore the company's specialty coffee products. It allows companies to share their brand history, sustainability ethos, roasting process, and work culture with users in an accessible and visually engaging manner.

What should a coffee shop website have?

Coffee shop websites should feature a good selection of high-resolution images that advertise their coffee products, work processes, and shop environment so visitors have a good sense of the space before they visit. It should also include a menu of the food and drinks available, as well as easily accessible contact options.
