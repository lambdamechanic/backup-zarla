Life Coach Website Examples - 9 Sites That Build Client Trust

Professional life coach website examples that are informative and engaging.

The best life coaching websites are inspiring and engaging. They incorporate detailed images and descriptions of the coach or coaching team with the type of services offered, how they can transform the lives of clients, and testimonials from satisfied people. Madison Arnholt

https://www.madisonarnholt.com/

Alexandria, Virginia, U.S.

Madison Arnholt is an intuitive life coach who endeavors to help clients find inner peace, purpose, and happiness through meditation and self-intuition practices. Her website reflects her commitment to helping others with wise words and links for free alignment bookings, a free trial of a meditation app, and a free subscription to her newsletter.

Credibility is provided by showcasing the logos of reputable companies who have worked with Madison, although the site would benefit from a dedicated testimonials page. A notable feature is the news interview with Madison on Good Morning Washington.

Pros and Cons

The website provides various free resources to boost engagement and performs well on WAVE. An interactive calendar allows people to choose their preferred booking time.

Some of the text obscures the images and hamburger menu when reading on mobile. The website does not have a dedicated testimonials page.

Desktop Review

On desktop devices, the large images take center stage, showcasing Madison's beaming smile while pertinent information is neatly filled into elegantly designed text blocks. Although all the information is easily accessible from the top menu, it quickly disappears when scrolling, requiring users to manually scroll back to the top for further navigation.

In our tests, the desktop site loaded quickly, although the video interview has an autoplay feature that creates slightly slower loading times for the main page. The site scored 75/100 for performance on Google's PageSpeed Insights tests.

Mobile Review

The mobile website was slightly slower and didn't quite emulate the balance of text and images seen on the desktop version. The promotional banner on the home page is more prominent, becoming almost intrusive, and some of the text obscures the images of Madison. As with the desktop site, the top menu doesn't float on the screen.

While the mobile site scored 29/100 for performance on Google's PageSpeed Insights test, real-world experience and navigation fell within acceptable limits.

DreamWithDan

https://dreamwithdan.com/

Cabo, Baja California Sur, Mexico

Daniel Mangena is a successful entrepreneur and life coach who teaches clients how to manifest wealth and live an abundant, purpose-driven life. His website is a visual spectacle of dreamy animation that looks appealing, but the animations are slightly distracting and make the website CPU-heavy and slow to load.

Daniel's home page is well thought out with a detailed description of his coaching program and clear links to his podcast and blog. A key feature of the site is the testimonials section of the home page where visitors can find a few video interviews from satisfied clients.

Pros and Cons

The website is engaging and visually appealing. It's easy to find information about Daniel's podcast, upcoming events, and speaking opportunities.

The background animations make the site slow to load and CPU-heavy. WAVE also picked up a number of contrast errors. Clicking on the promotional quiz banner directs users off the site.

Desktop Review

On larger screens, the website is visually spectacular but, despite the high-contrast text, we found the background animations slightly distracting, making it difficult to digest the text on the page. The main menu doesn't float down the page, making navigation difficult. The coaching offering link is hidden at the bottom of the page.

Confirming what we saw on our real-world tests, the desktop site scored 58/100 on the PageSpeed Insights test.

Mobile Review

While slower, the mobile website has a better layout with larger images and less distracting background animations. Unlike the desktop version, the mobile site has an additional bottom menu that floats as you scroll. The mobile website scored 30/100 for performance on Google's PageSpeed Insights test but fared better on SEO with a score of 92/100.

Happen to Your Career

https://happentoyourcareer.com/

Moses Lake, Washington, U.S.

Happen to Your Career is a career coaching company that provides targeted interview and career coaching for young professionals. The colorful website is designed with young professionals in mind and includes helpful sections such as the "I'm New Here" and "Resources" pages, as well as an unobtrusive chat widget for additional assistance.

While the website doesn't have a dedicated testimonials page, there are dozens of success stories on the "Coaching" page that visitors can filter by location, title, or product. The website also includes live links to the latest podcast episodes and detailed team bios.

Pros and Cons

There are detailed success stories on the "Coaching" page. Live podcast links can be found on the home page.

The "Product Sponsorship" link on the home page is broken and WAVE picked up a number of contrast errors. The "Book" menu link could be misinterpreted as a place to book a session.

Desktop Review

On larger screens, the visually appealing stock images and quirky text provide a splash of style, but on some screens the expansive white space on the edges of the page means the text is squeezed into the center, making the page feel unnecessarily crowded. The desktop site scored 92/100 for performance on Google's PageSpeed Insights test, confirming the fast loading times we found in our real-world test.

Mobile Review

The mobile site is slow to load and cumbersome to navigate, with one or two pages requiring side-scrolling to view all the content. The hamburger menu at the top of the page is the only navigation tool that quickly disappears as users scroll down. The mobile site scored 33/100 for performance on Google's PageSpeed Insights test.

Rebecca Kirk

https://rebeccakirk.co.uk/

Wilmslow, Cheshire, U.K.

Rebecca Kirk is a career and life coach who provides holistic coaching services for people on a spiritual path. Rebecca's website has a personal focus with plenty of images of her and a dedicated page about her life, purpose, and awakening journey.

Through the various top menu links, visitors can easily find information about the different coaching options on offer, client testimonials, and contact details. The website also includes a host of helpful resources including a career change quiz, a business energy audit, and a new business launch test.

Pros and Cons

The website includes a host of helpful resources. The testimonials page includes personal stories and a link to Google reviews, which establish trust.

WAVE picked up a number of low-contrast links. Some of the images look fuzzy on larger screens.

Desktop Review

The desktop website is fast and uncluttered but lacks a sticky navigation menu. The larger screen size allows for more focus on the images but also means the lower-quality images appear pixelated. The desktop site scored 96/100 for performance on Google's PageSpeed Insights test, confirming the fast response times found in our real-world test.

Mobile Review

Rebecca Kirk's mobile website scales well and loads as quickly as the desktop version. Unlike the desktop site, the mobile version includes a floating top menu for quick navigation. On smaller screens, the low-quality stock images look sharper and more professional. The mobile site scored 88/100 for performance on Google's PageSpeed Insights test.

Navigate Life Coaching

https://navigatelifecoach.com/

Covent Garden, London, U.K.

Navigate Life Coach, run by certified Master Life Coach, Sarisha Naidoo, offers personalized coaching services in London, including life, business, corporate coaching, and public speaking. The website has a professional, almost corporate aesthetic that reflects the type of coaching on offer.

The "Why Choose Me" section is a nice feature where visitors can read Sarisha's backstory, find out what she offers, and read through the FAQs. Another key feature is the scrolling review bar on the home page with Google reviews. These are complemented by the personal stories on the "Success Stories" page.

Pros and Cons

The website includes Google reviews and success stories. Sarisha has a dedicated "Why Choose Me" section. There is a floating WhatsApp button on the desktop and mobile site.

WAVE picked up a number of contrast issues. The top menu does not float on the page.

Desktop Review

The desktop website benefits from larger background visuals, however, the site seems to be more adapted for smaller screens as the text and top menu are confined to the center of the page. The top menu also disappears when scrolling, making navigation difficult. The desktop site loaded quickly in our real-world test and scored 75/100 for performance on Google's PageSpeed Insights test.

Mobile Review

The mobile website felt slower than the desktop version, and since it requires more scrolling, needs a back-to-top button or sticky menu. The background images are also less prominent and less effective on a smaller screen. The mobile site scored 38/100 for performance on Google's PageSpeed Insights test but scored over 90 for SEO and best practices.

Jason Snaddon

https://www.jasonsnaddon.com/

Sydney, New South Wales, Australia

Jason Snaddon, known as the "Abundance Activator," is a life coach specializing in helping clients achieve abundance in various areas of life, including relationships, health, career, and personal purpose.

Jason's website is effective in providing potential clients with all the information they need to know about him, his coaching style, and what he offers. There are multiple videos on the site presented by Jason as well as a blog and an online shop.

Pros and Cons

The website includes a blog, a bookings page, and an online shop. Visitors can find information about divorce support.

The website does not have a dedicated testimonials page. WAVE picked up dozens of contrast errors on the site. Pricing is difficult to find.

Desktop Review

The desktop site looks great on larger screens with a good text-to-image ratio that keeps the site engaging but easy to digest. The numerous videos on the site loaded quickly, and overall we felt the site was fast. The desktop site scored 79/100 on the PageSpeed Insights test.

Mobile Review

On mobile devices, the images and text scale well, but the embedded videos need to be opened in full-page view to get the desired effect. While the mobile site scored 34/100 on Google's PageSpeed Insights test, the loading times fell within acceptable limits in our real-world test.

Clarity Coaching

https://claritycoaching.ca/

Ontario, Canada

Clarity Coaching is a life coaching service based in Ontario. The company, led by certified life coach Erin Snively, aims to help individuals transform their lives from merely surviving to thriving. Erin's website is as informative as it is engaging with a detailed description of the benefits of life coaching, a blog page, FAQs, and testimonials.

A notable feature of the website is the "Coaching Topics" page where Erin provides details about the various topics discussed during life coaching sessions. The home page also includes a pricing section where visitors can book a free 30-minute discovery call.

Pros and Cons

The website explains the difference between therapy and life coaching, which might help visitors ascertain if the services meet their needs. There were almost no errors on the WAVE report.

There is no dedicated page for testimonials. The top menu bar is a bit small for desktop users.

Desktop Review

The desktop website loads quickly and has a good visual presence, although the text of the top menu bar could be much larger. The typeface used for the text is stylish but not well-defined against the background, which could be difficult for people with visual impairments to read. The desktop website scored 97/100 for performance on Google's PageSpeed Insights test.

Mobile Review

The mobile website adapts well to smaller screens, although the text is a bit too small in some areas and a few of the pages load slower than on the desktop site. Navigation is more difficult on mobile as the site is missing the back-to-top button that is featured on the desktop version.

Confirming the slightly slower loading times we found in our real-world test, the mobile website scored 68/100 for performance on the PageSpeed Insights test.

FAQs:

What should a life coach website look like?

Life coach websites should look professional, inviting, and confident. There should be easily identifiable links for qualifications, bookings, reviews/testimonials, and a dedicated page about the coach and what they have to offer.

How do I start a life coach website?

Use Zarla's AI website builder to create a professional, stylish, and SEO-optimized website in minutes.

What are some life coach website examples?

See our curated collection of professional life coach websites with a review of the desktop and mobile experience. Our favorite examples include DreamWithDan and Navigate Life Coaching.

What do the best life coaching websites include?

  • High-quality images of the coach or coaching team.
  • A detailed page about the type of coaching on offer.
  • An "About Me" page describing their journey to becoming a life coach, with qualifications.
  • A call-to-action button(s) on the home page directing readers to introductory courses or free consultations.
  • A testimonials page with detailed reviews from satisfied clients.
